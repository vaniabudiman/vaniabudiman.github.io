Categories

  • Communication: imparting or interchange of thoughts, opinions, or information by speech, writing, or signs.
  • Culture: behaviours and beliefs characteristic of a particular social, ethnic, or age group.
  • Creativity: ability to transcend traditional ideas, rules, patterns, relationships, or the like, and to create meaningful new ideas, forms, methods, interpretations, etc.; originality, progressiveness, or imagination.
  • Research: diligent and systematic inquiry or investigation into a subject in order to discover or revise facts, theories, applications, etc.
  • Logic: science that investigates the principles governing correct or reliable inference.

How to use SSC Electives Explorer

  1. You can search for specific electives on the left panel and we will return a list of electives that meet your specifications.
  2. Once you find an elective that you're interested in, you can click on Preview Skill Profile. This will allow you to see how the elective will affect your skills profile, which is shown on the right panel.
  3. Blue indicates the skills that you already have, and red indicates the skills that you can add on to your skillset if you were to take the elective.
  4. Each category is broken down to skills (e.g. communication is broken down into writing and collaboration).
